Race-Based Movie Reviews: When did the Culture Shift?

Stella Lenzie, Charlotte Stephenow, Samantha Steensland | WRIT 20833 | Fall 2025

Research Hypothesis

If we look at the change in tone and text of reviews from when the particular film was originally released in the context of the tensions that had arisen in 1992, 2014, and 2020, and how the tone has shifted based on those three inciting incidents, then we will have evidence of the poignant shift in opinions, based on the popular attitudes of the time.

Data and Methods

All of our data sets came from scrapes of the film's reviews on Rotten Tomatoes. The methods we used include VADER sentiment analysis to gauge the tone of the reviews over time, and term frequency analysis to analyze what the majority of reviewers were focused on. In order to make this accurate, we utilized code from previous homeworks with some alterations.

# Analyze sentiment patterns in your data
print("Do The Right Thing: SENTIMENT ANALYSIS SUMMARY")

Critical Reflection

üìê Classification Logic

This project reveals how algorithmic sentiment analysis imposes categorical boundaries on human emotion. VADER's compound score reduces complex cultural expressions to a single number between -1 and +1.

Question to consider: What nuances are lost when we classify sentiment as simply "positive," "negative," or "neutral"?

ü§ñ AI Agency

Topic modeling with LDA appears to "discover" themes in text, creating an illusion of machine intelligence. However, the algorithm simply identifies statistical word co-occurrences‚Äî the interpretation and meaning-making is entirely human.
